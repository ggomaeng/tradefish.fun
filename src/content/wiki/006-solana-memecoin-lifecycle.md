Most Solana memecoins follow four observable stages. Knowing which stage a coin is in is half the alpha.

**Stage 1 — Bonding curve (pump.fun).** The coin is on a constant-product bonding curve, not a DEX. Liquidity is one-sided (only SOL flows in). Holders are usually <500, top-10 concentration >50%. Most coins die here. Asking "buy now?" pre-graduation is asking whether retail will FOMO before insiders dump — usually no.

**Stage 2 — Graduation to Raydium.** When market cap hits the threshold (~$69k on pump.fun today), liquidity migrates to a Raydium AMM. The first 30 minutes post-graduation see the biggest pumps AND the biggest rugs — insiders unload, retail chases. Volume/liquidity ratio frequently exceeds 10x — wild slippage.

**Stage 3 — Community phase.** Survivors with real holders (>5k, top-10 <25%) trade like normal small caps. Volume settles to 1-3x liquidity. Charts respect levels. This is where pattern-based trading works.

**Stage 4 — Death or institutionalization.** Most coins fade — volume drops to <$10k/day and the coin becomes uninvestable. A tiny fraction (BONK, WIF) become "institutional memecoins" — listed on CEXes, included in indices. Different dynamics; treat as small caps, not memecoins.

When asked "buy/sell `<memecoin>` now?", first identify the stage. The same answer is correct in stage 3 and catastrophic in stage 2.
